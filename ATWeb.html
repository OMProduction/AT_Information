The Appalacian Trail

A Footpath For Those Who Seek Fellowship With the Wilderness

The Appalacian Trail is a national trail that is about 2,193 miles long. It starts in Georgia and makes its way up to Maine. Many people decide to hike chunks of the appalachain trail, but some thru-hike the whole 2,000 miles.

Grandma Gatewood:

She was a 67 Year old Great-grand-mother who had 11 kids. She left with 200 dollars and walked the whole trail in just her shoes and a bag. She finished the trail surviving a rattlesnake attack, two hurricanes, and a run in with Harlem Gangsters. Animals On The Appalacian

The Appalaccian Trail has a high variation and assortment of animals icluding more common to some you might not even notice. Here is some of the animals the trail holds.

  1. Mice

    2. Mice are in all parts of the trail. There is a very low chance you wont see a mice if you ever hike the trail. They are commonly found in the trail shelters.

  2. Rattlesnakes

    3. Though rattlesnakes might seem dangerous they actually help prevent lime disease by preying on the Mice who contract lime disease to the Ticks. Rattlesnakes are also mainly found in 1 part of the trail

  3. Ticks

    4. Ticks are the most dangerous animal/insect on the trail. Even with there small size they carry lime disease which if not treated in humans can cause Paralization, or even death.

  4. Horses

    5. Horses are one of the rarest animals on the trail infact they should not be there. May people think they escaped and after decades now there are a small ggroup of wild horses on the trail

  5. Bears

    6. Bears are a common sight nywhere on the trail some sheltters offer a uley system to haul your food into a tree so the bears dont take it. Though there are many encounters there are very few reported deaths.

  6. Elk

    7. In the Smokey Mountian part of the trail you can encounter wild elk traveling in groups.

Tips & Facts

  • Tip- Stay lower in elevation: make weather estimation and planning easier.
  • Fact- Vermont is a highly elevated part of the trail, ranging from 400, 4000 ft.
